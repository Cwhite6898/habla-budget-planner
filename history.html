⚠ Context & Caution

FY20 & FY21: The COVID Years

These two fiscal years were profoundly disrupted by the COVID-19 pandemic. Their numbers are preserved here for historical reference but should be treated with extreme caution in any budget modeling.

FY20 (Jul 2019 – Jun 2020)
Partial Year — Gala Survived
$76,639
The spring Gala happened — and was our biggest Gala ever at $40,805. This is a valid planning benchmark: the community had no awareness of COVID, the event ran normally, and the outcome reflects genuine fundraising capacity. Schools closed in March 2020 just after the Gala. Caminata was cancelled ($1,200 in pre-sales only). Corporate matching surged to $21,100 — likely pandemic-related generosity, so treat that line item separately.
FY21 (Jul 2020 – Jun 2021)
Nearly Full Shutdown
$47,658
School was fully remote for ~44 of 50 weeks. No Gala, no Caminata. The Read-a-thon ($19,771) was the standout fundraiser — a virtual format that worked well with kids at home. Buy-a-Spot, donations, corporate matching, merchandise, and Giving Tuesday added another ~$23K. (Yearbook ~$4,500 nets to zero.) Despite low activity, HABLA continued supporting the community: Teacher Reimbursement Grants ($8,508), Teacher Appreciation ($1,857), Student Scholarships ($630), Clubs & Activities ($3,438), Food Drive ($400), plus admin. The $23,196 surplus reflects dramatically reduced activity — not inactivity.

⚠ Budget Planning Note: FY20 Caminata and FY21 data should be excluded or handled carefully in budget analysis. The FY20 Gala ($40,805) is a valid benchmark — it ran as a normal event with no COVID awareness. However, FY20 Corporate Matching ($21,100) was likely pandemic-elevated and should not be used as a baseline. FY21's low expenses created a misleading surplus — the school was closed. Use FY17–FY20 Gala and FY22–FY25 as your Gala planning range; exclude FY20–21 Caminata entirely.
Programs · Vendor History

Art & Music: Our Vendors Over Time

Each era brought a different approach to arts programming — from parent-run volunteer models to professional vendor partnerships.

🎨
Art Program
FY17 – FY20 · 2016–2020
Art In Action
Parent Volunteer Program
AIA was a curriculum-based parent-led visual arts program where trained parent volunteers delivered structured lessons in every classroom. HABLA paid a licensing fee (~$3,600/yr) for curriculum materials and parents provided the labor. Income line 4066 reflects program participation fees. Suspended during COVID.
FY21 – FY22 · 2020–2022
Transition / Reduced
Gap / COVID + Recovery
No art program in FY21. FY22 saw art supplies purchased ($6,910) with reduced scope — likely volunteer-driven with no formal vendor.
FY23 – FY24 · 2022–2024
Art House
Professional Vendor
Art House brings professional artists into the school. Flat-fee contract: $13,500 (FY23), $14,790 (FY24). Higher cost than AIA licensing but no parent volunteer coordination required.
FY25 · 2024–2025
School Funded Year
School Absorbed Cost
The school district paid for the full art program in FY25 — HABLA contributed $0. This was a funding shift, not a program gap. Art House continued; the school simply picked up the tab that year. HABLA resumed co-funding in FY26.
FY26 · 2025–Present
Art House Returns
Professional Vendor
Art House continues with a cost-sharing model — HABLA pays $15,900 (half the total cost) and the school covers the other half. A sustainable split that keeps Art House in the school while reducing HABLA's full burden.
🎵
Music Program
FY17 – FY22 · 2016–2022
Maestro Santos
Longtime Music Teacher
Maestro Santos served K–2 throughout his tenure. In FY17 only, HABLA also ran a separate vendor for grades 3–5, which is why music costs that year spiked to $28,110 (also included musical theater at $15,631). In all other Santos years the cost was a steady ~$13–15K for K–2 alone. Paused during COVID. Returned post-COVID at $6,467 (FY22).
FY23 – FY24 · 2022–2024
Suzy Dorn
Professional Vendor
Suzy Dorn significantly expanded the music program — costs rose sharply to $19,579 (FY23) and $30,520 (FY24). The FY24 cost of $30,520 is the highest music spend in HABLA's history. Likely indicates a broader or more intensive program scope.
FY25 · 2024–2025
School Funded Year
School Absorbed Cost
The school district paid for the full music program in FY25 — HABLA contributed just $175. HABLA brought in Lara Lopez from Willow Glen Elementary for K–2. Same school cost-sharing shift as art that year. Coincided with the transition away from Suzy Dorn.
FY26 · 2025–Present
Individuo
New Music Vendor
Individuo is the new music program provider. HABLA's share in FY26 is $1,800 for content creation — the school funds the remainder of the program. A much leaner cost structure than the Suzy Dorn era ($19–30K). First full year — watch to see if scope/cost expands.
Also FY26
Musical Theatre subsidy returns — absent since FY18, HABLA resumed a $5,000 subsidy toward the school's musical theatre production in FY26. HABLA is not running the production, just supporting it.
Program Investment

School Program Spending

HABLA's investment in the school has grown significantly — and diversified. What started as art, music, and recess has expanded into a much broader portfolio of programs, grants, and student support.

Total HABLA Spending on School — All Categories
Stacked by category · FY20–21 COVID years shown but should not be used as benchmarks · Library remodel inflates FY17–18
FY17 – FY18
The Library Years
A two-year library remodel (~$61K total) inflates these numbers significantly. Strip that out and core program spending was actually modest — art, music, recess, and teacher grants running at a reasonable steady-state.
FY19 – FY22
Disrupted Years
These years are hard to use as a baseline. FY19 is missing some program payments likely due to timing. FY20 had a cancelled Caminata. FY21 was COVID shutdown. FY22 was the first year back with a scavenger hunt instead of a full Caminata. A full normal operating year — programs running, both fundraisers at scale — was running closer to $115–126K even before the expansion.
FY23 – FY24
The Expansion Era
Fueled by strong post-COVID fundraising, HABLA deliberately invested more. Science workshops were added ($21K), enrichment scholarships grew, and teacher appreciation doubled. Spending nearly doubled from FY22's $77K to FY24's $146K — a conscious choice, not an accident.
FY25 – Present
A New Partnership
The school began co-funding programs — art and music in FY25, art in FY26 — so HABLA's dollar figure looks lower on paper. But programs continue, and teacher grants hit an all-time high of $18,909 in FY25. The relationship with the school has matured.

🔭 Items to Watch for FY27 Planning
⚠️
Caminata FY25 drop: From $50K (FY23–24) to $30K (FY25) is a 41% decline. Was it the format? Date? Participation levels? Weather? Answering this before budgeting is critical. If it was a one-time factor, $45K is achievable. If structural, budget $35K.
⚠️
Gala FY25 softness: $46.7K after two years at $68–74K. Venue, theme, and top-of-ticket items drive Gala income. A strong "wow" factor can recover this to $65K+. Recommend post-mortem on FY25 Gala before locking FY27 target.
🎵
Individuo (new music vendor): HABLA paid only $1,800 in FY26 for music creation and spring concert — the school covers the rest. This is a dramatically leaner model than Suzy Dorn's $19-30K range. Confirm what HABLA's share will be in FY27 and whether the school cost-sharing arrangement continues.
🎨
Art & Music school funding: In FY25, the school absorbed both art and music entirely. In FY26, HABLA resumed art ($15,900) while the school still co-funds music. This cost-sharing dynamic is new and worth formalizing — understanding what the school will vs. won't continue to fund is key to FY27 budgeting.
✨
Corporate Matching opportunity: This line item has been chronically under-budgeted. With FY26 already at $10.75K against a $5K budget, the FY27 budget should be at minimum $10K. Consider a dedicated matching drive campaign — the ROI is exceptional.
📊
Buy-a-Spot declining: The FY23 peak of $13.5K has not been repeated. FY24–25 averaged $5K. The program may need a relaunch with new catalog items, better promotion, or integration into the Caminata event experience to revive.
